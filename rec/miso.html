Soup recipe

decription

Miso soup is a classic Japanese soup made from a savory dashi broth mixed with miso paste, often filled with tofu, seaweed, and green onions. It's warm, comforting, and takes only a few minutes to make at home.

ingredents

  • 2 cups (480 ml) dashi broth
  • 1½-2 tbsp miso paste
  • ½ cup soft tofu, cut into small cubes
  • 1-2 tbsp dried wakame seaweed
  • 1-2 green onions, thinly sliced

steps

  1. Place dried wakame in a small bowl of water for 5 minutes to rehydrate, then drain.
  2. Pour the dashi into a small pot and gently bring it to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Add the tofu cubes and softened wakame to the simmering broth. Heat for 1-2 minutes.
  4. In a small ladle or bowl, take a little of the hot broth and dissolve the miso paste in it.
  5. Turn off the heat and gently stir the dissolved miso mixture back into the pot.
  6. Add sliced green onions, taste, and adjust with a bit more miso if needed. Serve warm.
